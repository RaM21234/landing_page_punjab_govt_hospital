Services Provided

Diagnostic Ultrasound:

Hospitals provide diagnostic ultrasound services to visualize internal body structures, including organs, blood vessels, and tissues. This type of ultrasound is used to identify conditions like gallstones, kidney stones, tumors, and fetal development during pregnancy.

Learn More

Obstetric Ultrasound:

This specialized ultrasound focuses on monitoring the development and well-being of the fetus during pregnancy. It can detect any potential issues and provide valuable insights into the baby's health.

Learn More

Gynecological Ultrasound:

Gynecological ultrasounds are used to examine the female reproductive system, including the uterus, ovaries, and fallopian tubes. They help diagnose conditions such as ovarian cysts, fibroids, and reproductive health issues.

Learn More

Abdominal Ultrasound:

Abdominal ultrasounds examine the organs in the abdomen, including the liver, pancreas, spleen, and kidneys. They are used to detect abnormalities, such as masses, cysts, or infections.

Learn More

Cardiac Ultrasound:

Hospitals offer echocardiograms to assess the heart's structure and function. This type of ultrasound helps diagnose heart conditions, such as valve problems, cardiomyopathy, and congenital heart defects.

Learn More

Vascular Ultrasound:

Vascular ultrasounds use Doppler technology to visualize blood flow through arteries and veins. They help diagnose conditions like deep vein thrombosis (DVT), arterial blockages, and varicose veins.
